Our Story

A motherhood journey

As a mother, I was looking for dresses for my daughter that were both beautiful and comfortable for her sensitivity. Many children's garments had intermediate cuts, visible seams and stiff fabrics that didn't allow her to feel calm and free.

From that search, we began designing with well-being and comfort in mind. We tested different fabrics and structures until we found what truly worked: dresses made of 100% lightweight, breathable cotton, without intermediate cuts, with fewer seams, hidden seams and fabrics that don't scratch, prioritizing well-being without sacrificing design.

Beyond comfort, we wanted each garment to tell a story. We created characters inspired by Peru, connecting children with their culture and conveying positive messages through simple stories, sparking curiosity, identity and learning.

Characters with meaning

Each illustration tells a part of our biodiversity.

Amaru, the playful jaguar

In the jungles of Peru, Amaru leaps between colors and dreams. He doesn't run to scare, he runs to play. Amaru teaches us that the greatest strength is joy.

Delfi, the pink dolphin

In a river in the Peruvian jungle lives Delfi with her mom. Delfi is pink and small, but very brave. One day she learned that what matters isn't how you look, but how you feel inside. Delfi teaches us that being different is also a strength.

Lloqui, the Peruvian dog

On the coast of Peru lives Lloqui, a dog with a superpower. With his skin he feels the sun, the wind and caresses more than anyone, guiding those around him. Lloqui teaches us that feeling differently is a talent that can illuminate and help others.

Pepito, the brave guinea pig

In the Peruvian highlands, Pepito the guinea pig is small, but his bravery is enormous. When everyone feels fear, he dares to look closer. Pepito teaches us that even if you're small, you can be brave and learn to see others with your heart.

Piki, the Peruvian hummingbird

Piki wakes up early to greet the sun. With his tiny flight he fills the air with colors. Piki teaches us that even the smallest can make the world great.

Chuy, the creative llama

In the Peruvian highlands lives Chuy, a llama who one day discovered her talent. With her wool she learned to create sweaters that warm others in the cold. Chuy teaches us that following your dreams and using your talents can change your path and help others.
